Frequently Asked Questions by Newcomers in Belgium

THIS DOCUMENT IS NOT INTENDED TO PROMOTE OR FAVOUR ANY PARTICULAR COMMERCIAL OR BUSINESS ACTIVITIES.
This page is put together purely based on the experiences shared by our Malayali Community who have been living in Belgium over the past few years. This is to make sure that every malayali planning to settle in Belgium for education/work/ family/ or any other reasons get to familiarise and adapt to life in Belgium.

How do I connect with Malayalees in Belgium?

No matter which end of the world you go to, you will find a Malayali. Likewise, here in Belgium, we have a super friendly and welcoming community- Kairali Belgium Malayali Association.
Kairali is a non profit association that was formed primarily to support and stay connected with the fellow malayalis living in Belgium. The association is very active and organises various cultural and sports events regularly.

Become a member

You can join the group using the following link. https://kairalibe-mem-registration.web.app/

For Updates/ queries

You can stay updated with the upcoming events by following Kairali Belgium Malayali Association group on facebook on the following link. https://www.facebook.com/groups/kairalibe/
For any queries you can directly send a message on the facebook group and you should normally get a reply within 24 hours.

Here is a list of useful websites to find a property/house/apartment for rent
  1. https://www.immoweb.be/ (Search for huur with specific location)
  2. https://www.era.be
  3. https://www.immoscoop.be

Glossary: te huur = to rent, te koop= to buy, Huis = House, Appartement = Apartment, Locatie= location

Where can I buy Kerala food items?

  • Everest Leuven : Brusselsestraat 128, 3000 Leuven
  • Euro Asia Supermarket :Statiestraat 75, 2600 Antwerpen
A wide range of Kerala frozen food items like Kerala parotta ,kappa, green chillies, curry leaves, kumbilappam, parippuvada, pazhampori, ....all essential spices and condiments, rice, atta, and a variety of ready to eat or easy to prepare food items are available at these stores.
Tip: Search for a Srilankan store nearby and if you find one you are most likely to find similar products.

Where to buy the most affordable day to day essentials and grocery?

Most shops and stores close by 6.00 pm in Belgium. Don't be surprised to see your nearest supermarket closed by 6.00 pm.
  1. Colruyt (https://www.colruyt.be/nl)
    For milk, eggs, bananas, fresh vegetables, toilet cleaner, oil, tissue papers, bread, onion, beer and other alcoholic beverages. Colruyt has their own private label - Everyday and Boni. These brands are cheaper and economical most of the time.
  2. Action (https://www.action.com/)
    For economic stationery items like a Toilet cleaning brush, Buckets, USB Cable, notebooks, Pencil, Pen etc
  3. Kruidvat (https://www.kruidvat.be/)
    For good deals on beauty and self care products like Shampoo, Conditioner, Hair oil, cosmetics, sanitary pads etc.

How to open a Bank Account?

Most of the banks allows you to start a bank account without a Residence permit but you will need to submit your proof of employment (job contract).

Alternatively KBC has an option of starting a bank account without a residence permit or any other proof. Once the residence card has been issued, this needs to be updated to the system.

Can I drink tap water?

You can normally drink tap water, But it is very hard.

It's not recommended to drink directly regularly, instead you can buy a normal water filter in Amazon here is a link:

https://www.amazon.de/-/en/Graphite-Cartridges-Half-Year-Limescale-Chlorine/dp/B01MRROWEQ?th=1

Drinking water is also available at reasonable price at colruyt and delhaize.
